Protein is the one macronutrient most families consistently undereat. Not because they don't want it — but because the foods we default to when we're tired and rushed (pasta, sandwiches, cereal) tend to be carb-heavy and protein-light.

The fix isn't a complicated meal plan. It's a handful of high-protein go-to meals you can rotate through the week without thinking too hard.

## Why Protein Matters More Than You Think

Protein keeps you full longer, supports muscle maintenance, stabilizes blood sugar, and helps kids grow. The general guideline for adults is around 0.8g per pound of body weight — and most people fall short without even realizing it.

The good news: you don't need supplements or specialty foods. Regular whole foods, cooked simply, get you there.

## 5 High-Protein Family Meals Under 30 Minutes

### 1. Sheet Pan Chicken Thighs with Roasted Vegetables

Chicken thighs are forgiving, affordable, and packed with protein. Toss them with olive oil, garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add whatever vegetables you have — broccoli, bell peppers, zucchini, sweet potato. Roast at 425°F for 25 minutes.

One chicken thigh has roughly 25g of protein. A serving of two with vegetables lands the whole family above their protein targets with minimal effort.

### 2. Greek Yogurt Chicken Bowls

Marinate chicken breasts in Greek yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, and cumin for even 15 minutes (or overnight). Cook in a skillet, slice, and serve over rice or quinoa with cucumber, tomato, and a drizzle of tahini.

Greek yogurt marinade tenderizes the chicken and adds extra protein even before the chicken is counted.

### 3. Black Bean and Egg Tacos

This one takes under 15 minutes. Warm black beans with cumin and garlic. Scramble eggs separately. Layer both in a corn tortilla with avocado, salsa, and a squeeze of lime.

Per serving: approximately 22g of protein. Kids love building their own, which means they actually eat it.

### 4. Salmon with Quinoa and Spinach

Salmon is one of the highest-protein foods per ounce. Season with olive oil, salt, pepper, and lemon. Cook skin-side down in a hot skillet for 4 minutes per side. Serve over quinoa (itself a complete protein) with wilted spinach.

This meal sounds fancy. It takes 20 minutes.

### 5. Turkey and Vegetable Stir-Fry

Ground turkey cooks in 8 minutes and takes on any flavor you give it. Brown with ginger, garlic, and soy sauce. Add broccoli, snap peas, and carrots. Serve over rice.

This is the meal that converts even picky eaters because the sauce is so good. Add a little sesame oil and chili flakes at the end.

## The Protein Staples to Always Have On Hand

- **Eggs** — the fastest protein source in your kitchen
- **Canned chickpeas and black beans** — no prep, instant protein in any meal
- **Greek yogurt** — breakfast, marinade, sauce, dip
- **Frozen edamame** — toss into any grain bowl straight from frozen
- **Chicken thighs** — cheaper than breasts, harder to overcook, just as much protein

## Personalizing Protein to Your Family's Goals

Every person in your family has different protein needs based on their age, activity level, and health goals. A teenage athlete needs dramatically more protein than a 5-year-old. A parent trying to lose weight needs more protein to preserve muscle during a caloric deficit.
